Vanavihari is an initiative of the Government of Andhra Pradesh and is owned by the State Forest Department. It is maintained by the Community-Based Ecotourism (CBET) initiative at Maredumilli.

Vanavihari is governed by the forest policies of both the State and Union Governments of India. Its core purpose is to provide financial benefits and empowerment to local communities, promote responsible tourism in natural areas, and create a sustainable, equitable, community-based model that improves the living standards of indigenous host communities.

The goal is to instill the values of wildlife conservation among the general public while offering the best possible immersive experience for nature-loving tourists.

Vanavihari is the brand name under which CBET Maredumilli operates. CBET Maredumilli was formed by selecting members from Somireddypalem Vana Samrakshana Samithi (VSS), who were subsequently trained in the fundamentals of the hospitality industry. The CBET micro-plan is approved by the Divisional Forest Officer, Rampachodavaram, and forwarded to the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (HoFF), Andhra Pradesh, for sanction of funds.

A 15-member management committee is constituted with 50% representation of women. Financial accounts are maintained by the management committee through a joint account opened in the names of the Chairman of the Management Committee and the concerned Forest Section Officer, who serves as the Member Secretary of the committee. After achieving a sustainable stage or upon completion of five years of successful operation, 50% of the revenue generated is to be deposited into a joint account held by CBET and the Forest Department, to be utilized for ecosystem development. The remaining 50% is retained in the CBET account and utilized as decided by the CBET General Body.
